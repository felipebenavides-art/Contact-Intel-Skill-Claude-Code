Section 0

Commands vs. Skills in Claude Code

Understanding the difference — and why it matters for how we built this tool.

Commands Built-in

What it is Built-in actions hardcoded into Claude Code — things like /help, /clear, or /code-review.
How you invoke it Always explicit — type /command-name directly in chat.
Best for Discrete, immediate actions you trigger manually every time.
Token cost Always loaded into context, whether used or not.

Skills Custom

What it is Reusable, multi-step workflows you define — stored as SKILL.md files and loaded on demand.
How you invoke it Type /skill-name — or Claude loads it automatically when it detects relevance.
Best for Repeatable processes, checklists, and long instruction sets you'd otherwise paste into chat every time.
Token cost Lazy-loaded — zero cost until invoked.

What is /contact-intel?

A single skill that takes an account name and delivers everything you need to go from zero to a fully drafted outreach strategy — pulling live Salesforce data, enriching it with external contacts, producing ready-to-send emails and LinkedIn messages, and automatically scheduling a 7-day follow-up check with a Slack notification.

Section 2

The 11-Phase Workflow

Every phase runs in sequence. Full context carries through from Phase 1 to Phase 11 — no handoffs, no repeated queries.

1

Find & Confirm Account

Searches Salesforce by account name, surfaces matches for you to confirm, then immediately pulls all open opportunities tied to that account.

2

Pull Contact Intelligence

Runs five SOQL queries in a single pass: all contacts, task history, event history, email history (last 24 months), and opportunity contact roles.

3

ZoomInfo / External Contacts

Before building strategy, asks if you have a ZoomInfo export. Cross-references it against Salesforce, flags anyone not in CRM, and folds them into the strategy.

4

Discovery Questions

Asks three focused questions about products, sales stage, and context. Automatically skips any question Salesforce already answers — and tells you why.

5

Intel Summary

Delivers a structured brief: account snapshot, contacts grouped as Active / Fading / Cold / Cautious, reconnect angles, and missing personas to source externally.

6

Contact Hierarchy

Organizes all contacts into Tier 1 (Economic Buyers), Tier 2 (Champions), and Tier 3 (Influencers) — each with product alignment, strategic importance, and warm/cold status.

7

Contact Selection You choose

Presents a prioritized numbered table — Tier 2 first, ordered by status — so you select exactly which contacts to include before any outreach is planned or drafted. Territory contacts (★) and cautious contacts (⚠) are flagged.

8

Salesforce Solution Mapping

Recommends additional Salesforce products beyond what you mentioned, mapped to specific selected contacts with a business case tailored to the account's industry and size.

9

Outreach Sequence

A week-by-week plan for selected contacts: who to approach first, top-down vs. bottom-up strategy, warm-contact bridges, and natural re-entry angles for fading relationships.

10

Draft Outreach

Produces email + LinkedIn InMail for each selected contact — warm contacts reference prior relationship and last topic; cold contacts get a tailored market intro aligned to the rep's territory. Signed with the rep's name, title, and email auto-detected from Salesforce.

11

Automated Follow-Up Check Auto in 7 days

Schedules a Claude task firing in 7 days that re-queries Salesforce for responses, classifies each contact as ✅ Responded / ⏳ No response / ❓ Unknown, and sends you a Slack DM with a summary and recommended next steps.

Section 3

Key Design Principles

Every decision in this skill was made to save time, reduce noise, and keep the data honest.

⚡

Single Salesforce Pass

All data is queried once — no duplicate lookups, no re-running the same queries across two separate skills.

🔍

ZoomInfo-First Enrichment

External contacts are brought in before strategy is built, not as an afterthought — so the hierarchy reflects the full picture.

✋

You Choose Who Gets Drafted

A prioritized numbered table gives you full visibility before any outreach is planned or written. Nothing is drafted without your selection.

🧠

Skips What It Already Knows

Discovery questions are automatically dropped if Salesforce data already provides a clear answer — and the skill tells you what it skipped and why.

⏰

Automated 7-Day Follow-Up

A scheduled task fires in 7 days, re-checks Salesforce, and sends a Slack DM with response status and recommended next steps — no manual reminders needed.

🌎

Territory-Aware

At Phase 0 the skill asks the rep for their territory. From that point on, contacts in that region are flagged with ★ and prioritized throughout — automatically tailored to whoever is running it.

🚩

Data Quality Flags

Surfaces duplicate records, missing phone numbers, non-company emails, and stale titles — so you go into outreach with clean data.
